MGF

For X: $M_X(t)=E[e^{tX}]=\int e^{tx} f_X(x)\,dx$ (continuous) or $\sum e^{tx} p_X(x)$ (discrete).

Expectation

$E[X]=\int x f_X(x)\,dx$ or $\sum x p_X(x)$. Conditional: $E[X|Y=y]=\int x f_{X|Y}(x|y)dx$.

Transforms

For $Y=g(X)$ use CDF technique: $F_Y(y)=P(g(X)\le y)$, then differentiate. For monotone $g$, $f_Y(y)=f_X(g^{-1}(y))\cdot |d/dy(g^{-1}(y))|$.

Convolution (sum)

If $W=X+Y$ independent, $f_W(w)=\int f_X(x) f_Y(w-x)\,dx$.
